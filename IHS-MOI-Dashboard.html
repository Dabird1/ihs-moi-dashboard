Methodology & Definitions
¹ TAM (Total Addressable Market): The total annual exterior remodeling spend across all housing units in the metro area. Derived from MSA housing stock (US Census ACS 2023), owner-occupancy rates (residential) or multifamily/commercial building counts (commercial), multiplied by annual exterior remodeling participation rate and average spend per active household. National benchmarks sourced from IBISWorld, NAHB, and the Joint Center for Housing Studies (JCHS) Leading Indicators of Remodeling Activity (LIRA), indexed to local cost-of-living conditions.
² SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): The portion of TAM addressable by IHS service lines — roofing, siding, windows, doors, gutters, and insulation. Represents approximately 70% of residential TAM and 80% of commercial TAM. Excludes interior remodeling, HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, and other non-exterior categories.
³ SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): The revenue IHS could realistically capture at a given penetration assumption. Default baseline is 5% of SAM, representing a competent, established operator in a metro market. A 10% target reflects a market-leading position. Users may enter any custom percentage. SOM is not a forecast — it is a scenario modeling tool.
⁴ Our Revenue: Actual brand revenue reported from IHS brand CRM actuals for active markets. For target markets (no current operations), revenue reflects modeled estimates from penetration slider inputs only. Residential and commercial revenue are tracked separately and never blended.
⁵ Penetration %: Our Revenue as a percentage of SAM. Calculated as (Our Revenue ÷ SAM) × 100. Color coded: green = at or above SOM baseline (≥5%), yellow = approaching baseline (2–4.9%), red = below threshold (<2%), gray = target market with no operations.
⁶ Whitespace: The revenue opportunity remaining within the SAM after subtracting actual brand revenue. Calculated as SAM minus Our Revenue. Represents the total addressable opportunity IHS has not yet captured in this market and segment.
Sources: US Census Bureau ACS 2023, Joint Center for Housing Studies Harvard University, Home Improvement Research Institute, IBISWorld, NAHB, Yardi Matrix, CoStar.
